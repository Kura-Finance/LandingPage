A crypto debit card sounds simple until you ask the important question: **who actually holds your money?**

With most crypto cards, the answer is the card issuer — your assets are custodied, converted, and pooled before you ever tap to pay. Kura Card takes a different path.

## Self-custody meets Visa

Kura Card connects your **Kura Wallet** (Safe smart account on Base) to the Visa network — spending is authorized against your on-chain balance rather than a custodial exchange account.

Here's the flow at a high level:

1. You hold stablecoins or supported assets in your **Kura Wallet** (Safe smart account on Base)
2. When you pay with Kura Card, the required amount is authorized against your on-chain balance
3. The merchant receives a normal Visa settlement — you keep self-custody until the moment of spend

Kura never pools user funds into a company-owned account. Your assets remain in wallets you control via Privy MPC key management.

## Why this matters

**Counterparty risk** — If a custodial card provider freezes accounts or enters bankruptcy, your spending balance may be tied up in their estate. Self-custody spending limits that exposure to the transaction itself.

**Transparency** — You can verify your on-chain balance independently. No "trust our dashboard" required.

**Alignment** — Kura's business model isn't built on rehypothecating your deposits. We earn through subscriptions ([see pricing](/pricing)) and disclosed partner integrations — not by holding your money.

## Getting your card

Card issuance requires identity verification through regulated card partners — standard KYC for any Visa product. Once approved, you manage limits, freeze/unfreeze, and view activity inside the Kura app.

Supported regions and asset types may vary. Check the app for current availability in your country.
